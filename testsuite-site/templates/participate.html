Getting Involved

Thanks for being interested in testing reading systems! We’re thrilled to have your help and hope this page makes it easy for you to sign up and get all the information you need.

There are two test suites: Accessibility and EPUB3. Descriptions of the testing process for each is outlined below.

Accessibility Testing

Overview

The basic assumption of evaluating accessibility is that all reading systems should support reading with eyes, ears, and fingers. It should be possible for users to read the content of the document by:

  • Adjusting the display such as adjusting font size and color combination
  • Reading the text with screen readers, Voiceover, Talkback, Etc.
  • Reading the text with a refreshable Braille display
  • Reading with assistive technologies designed for persons with dyslexia or other disabilities.
  • Reading with a screen magnifier
  • To evaluate the accessibility of reading systems, test books have been prepared with sample content to carry out the tests. The test books walk testers through the various accessibility tests and provide instructions on how to determine if the reading system is passing or not.

If you have additional questions or concerns, please don’t hesitate to email our community manager

EPUB3 Testing

The EPUB3 test suite is designed around the EPUB3 specification. It tests many essential features, and although it is thorough, it is not comprehensive.

If you are interested in becoming an evaluator for EPUB3 testing, please contact BISG. Once you are registered, you can use this site to enter results. Your results will be stored in the central database and will be available for your retrieval but will not be published in the EPUB 3 Support Grid until you request it.

Then, once you have an account and are logged-in, refer to Instructions for Evaluators.

Even if you are not interested in becoming an evaluator, you are still encouraged to download the test suite materials to test your reading system.
